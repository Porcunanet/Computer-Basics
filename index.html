SESSION 1

Getting Started with Computers

Today we'll learn about computer parts,
the mouse, and the keyboard.

What is a Computer?

A computer is a helpful tool that can:

  • Help you stay in touch with family
  • Find information quickly
  • Manage photos and documents
  • Provide entertainment

Basic Computer Parts

Monitor
The screen where you see everything
Keyboard
For typing letters and numbers
Mouse
For pointing and clicking
Computer Tower
The "brain" that does the work

Using the Mouse

  • Hold it gently - Rest your hand comfortably
  • Move slowly - The pointer follows your movement
  • Left click - Press the left button once
  • Double-click - Press left button twice quickly
Remember: Take your time. There's no rush!

Important Keyboard Keys

  • Letter keys - For typing words
  • Space bar - The long key at the bottom
  • Enter - Moves to next line
  • Backspace - Deletes mistakes
SESSION 2

Understanding the Desktop

Today we'll explore the desktop,
open programs, and start typing!

What is the Desktop?

Think of it like the top of your real desk!

  • Icons - Small pictures that open programs
  • Taskbar - The bar at the bottom of the screen
  • Start/Menu button - Opens the main menu

Opening and Closing Programs

  • Double-click an icon to open a program
  • Click the X button to close a program
  • Minimize button makes it hide at the bottom
  • Maximize button makes it fill the screen
Don't worry - you can't break the computer by clicking!

Your First Document

Let's type a simple letter or note!

  • The blinking line shows where text appears
  • Type slowly and carefully
  • Use Backspace to fix mistakes
  • Always save your work!
SESSION 3

Exploring the Internet

Today we'll learn to browse the web
and search for information safely.

What is the Internet?

A worldwide network connecting millions of computers

  • Find information on any topic
  • Stay in touch with family and friends
  • Watch videos and read news
  • Shop from home

Your Web Browser

The program that lets you visit websites:

  • Address bar - Where you type website addresses
  • Back button - Returns to previous page
  • Forward button - Goes forward again
  • Home button - Returns to start page

Searching for Information

  • Go to Google or another search engine
  • Type your question in simple words
  • Click on results that look helpful
  • Try different words if you don't find it
Example: "weather in my city" or "chocolate cake recipe"

Internet Safety Tips

  • Never share personal information with strangers
  • Look for the padlock icon for secure sites
  • Be careful with "too good to be true" offers
  • Ask for help if something seems strange
When in doubt, ask!
SESSION 4

Email & Staying Connected

Today we'll learn to send and receive emails
and stay in touch with loved ones.

Understanding Email

Electronic mail - messages sent instantly online

  • Free and fast communication
  • Send messages to anyone with an email address
  • Attach photos and documents
  • Keep in touch with family easily

Parts of an Email

  • To: The recipient's email address
  • Subject: What your email is about
  • Message: Your actual message
  • Send button: Sends your email
Always check before you send!

Your Email Inbox

  • Inbox - Where new emails arrive
  • Sent - Emails you've sent
  • Trash - Deleted emails
  • Reply - Respond to an email
