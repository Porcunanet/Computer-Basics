Files, Folders & Organization

Learn to organize your digital life
just like organizing a filing cabinet!

Files vs Folders

üìÑ File
A single item:
‚Ä¢ A document
‚Ä¢ A photo
‚Ä¢ A video
‚Ä¢ A song
üìÅ Folder
A container that holds files:
‚Ä¢ Like a filing cabinet drawer
‚Ä¢ Keeps things organized
‚Ä¢ Can hold other folders too
Think of it like this: Files are papers, folders are the folders you put them in!

Where Files Live on Your Computer

  • Documents: Letters, bills, written files
  • Pictures: All your photos
  • Downloads: Things from the internet
  • Desktop: Quick access to files
These are the main "rooms" in your computer's house!

Creating Your Own Folders

Name folders clearly so you can find things later:

  • "Family Photos 2024"
  • "Medical Records"
  • "Recipes"
  • "Tax Documents 2024"
Good naming = Easy finding later!

Finding Lost Files

  • Use the Search box - type what you're looking for
  • Check Recent files - shows what you opened recently
  • Look in Downloads - for things from internet
  • Check the Desktop - maybe you saved it there
Can't find it? Use search! Type any word from the file name.
SESSION 2

Working with Photos & Documents

Today we'll organize photos,
do simple editing, and share them!

Getting Photos from Your Phone

  • Connect phone with USB cable
  • Computer will recognize your phone
  • Copy photos to Pictures folder
  • Organize by date or event
Your photos are safe on the computer - they won't disappear if phone breaks!

Organizing Your Photos

Create albums by event or date:

  • "Thanksgiving 2024"
  • "Grandchildren Visits"
  • "Garden Photos"
  • "Vacation - Florida 2024"
Delete blurry or duplicate photos - it's okay to let them go!

Simple Photo Editing

  • Rotate: Turn photo right-side up
  • Crop: Remove unwanted edges
  • Brightness: Make photo lighter or darker
  • Save as copy: Keep original safe!
Always keep the original photo - edit a copy instead!

Sharing Photos by Email

  • Look for the paperclip or "Attach" button
  • Find your photo in the folders
  • Click to select it
  • Click "Open" or "Attach"
  • Photo appears in your email
You can attach multiple photos at once!
SESSION 3

Video Calling Family & Friends

See and talk to loved ones
no matter how far away they are!

Video Calling Apps

Zoom
Great for group calls
Works on any device
Free for calls under 40 min
FaceTime
For Apple devices
(iPhone, iPad, Mac)
Very simple to use
Ask your family which one they use - then use the same one!

Before You Call: Test Your Setup

  • Camera: Can you see yourself?
  • Microphone: Can you hear yourself?
  • Speakers/Headphones: Can you hear others?
  • Lighting: Face a window or light source
Test before the call so you're ready when family calls!

Making a Video Call

  • Open the video calling app
  • Find the person's name or use a link
  • Click "Call" or "Join"
  • Your camera and microphone turn on
  • Wait for them to answer
  • Smile and wave!

Important Buttons During Calls

  • Mute button: Turns your microphone off/on
  • Video button: Turns your camera off/on
  • Volume: Makes call louder or quieter
  • End call button: Usually red, hangs up
If they can't hear you, check if you're muted (microphone has a line through it)

Watch Out for These Scams

üö® Prize Scams

Red Flags - Signs of a Scam

  • Urgent demands for money or information
  • Asking for passwords or Social Security number
  • Too good to be true offers
  • Poor spelling and grammar
  • Threats or pressure tactics
Remember: Real companies NEVER ask for your password!

If You Think It's a Scam

  • Don't click any links
  • Don't call phone numbers in the message
  • Don't send money or gift cards
  • Don't share personal information
  • Do ask a family member or friend
  • Do report it and delete it
When in doubt, close it out! Better safe than sorry.

Creating Strong Passwords

Good passwords have:

  • At least 12 characters
  • Mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Not your birthday or pet's name
  • Different password for each important account
Example: MyGarden2024! or BlueBird$Sun7

Useful Websites to Explore

  • Weather.com - Weather forecasts
  • YouTube.com - How-to videos, music
  • Google Maps - Directions anywhere
  • Medicare.gov - Health insurance info
  • Your library website - Free ebooks!

Online Shopping Safety

  • Look for the padlock icon in address bar
  • Stick to known retailers (Amazon, Walmart, Target)
  • Read reviews before buying
  • Understand return policies
  • Avoid deals that seem too cheap
If a website looks suspicious or unprofessional, don't buy from it!
